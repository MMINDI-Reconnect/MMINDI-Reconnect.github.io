Draft Agenda

*subject to change - 2016.02.21

Friday Evening -- Meet and Greet

  • Check in to the La Posada Hotel, and then join us at the indoor/ outdoor bar next to the Staab House restaurant for a meet and greet w/appetizers. (Time: 6:00 P.M. to ?)

$35 Friday Night Event; there will be cash bar.


Saturday Day -- On your own - List of possible activities you might be interested in...


Trolley Tour

  • Santa Fe tours for $15.00 per person or/and do any of the following on-your-own. Note: if enough interest will work out a group.

Canyon Road

  • Canyon Road is one of Santa Fe's most beautiful treasures, featuring art galleries of every kind. Walk and shop through a seemingly endless representation of art genres, including sculpture gardens and modern works. When you get to the top of the hill, have a margarita at Geronimo or El Farol. Currently on their schedule is the Santa Fe Farmer's Market for the Saturday we're there.

Raft the Taos Box

  • The Santa Fe Rafting Company hosts all day trips down the Rio Grande through the Taos Box. Trips depart at 9:00 A.M. and return at approximately 5:30 P.M. Lunch is served on the river. This past season the price was $99.00 per person. *note: this isn't organized by us, this is on your own!

    Phone: (505) 988-4914

Ten Thousand Waves

  • Santa Fe's premier Japanese Spa offers massage, facials, and public or private tubs. Stay for a couple of hours or relax all day at this exceptional outdoor treasure.

    Call and reserve a treatment: (505) 982-9304


Saturday Evening -- Casual dinner at Cafe Fina!

  • The shuttle will leave La Posada at 7:00 P.M to Cafe Fina. After dinner, we will regroup at "The Palace Saloon" for drinks and dancing.

$60.00; includes bus, dinner, and tips; excluding alchohol


Sunday Afternoon -- On your own

  • Enjoy brunch on your own at La Posada, then hang out at the pool, shop or enjoy beautiful Santa Fe on foot or by pedi-cab.

If you arrive early or stay late, there are many more options for nearby excursions to places like Tent Rocks, Madrid, Taos, Abiquiu Lake, and Nambe Falls.*
